A system waveguide couples to N bath modes with random phase offsets. With few modes, energy oscillates back and forth (Poincaré recurrence). Increase N toward a continuum and the recurrence time diverges — the system amplitude decays exponentially, recovering Fermi's golden rule. This is the coupled-mode-theory origin of irreversibility in quantum mechanics.
