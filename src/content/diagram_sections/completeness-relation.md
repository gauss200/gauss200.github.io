The completeness relation Σₙ |n⟩⟨n| = Î states that the eigenstates of a measurement operator span the full Hilbert space. In the retrocausal loop picture each eigenstate |n⟩ corresponds to a closed amplitude loop: the forward wavefunction ψₙ propagates from the shared initial boundary condition to the final eigenstate, while its conjugate ψₙ* returns along the time-reversed path to close the loop. All N loops depart from the same initial condition but terminate at disjoint final boundary conditions — the measurement eigenstates. When a measurement is performed, one loop is selected as the real, ontic trajectory; the remaining loops are counterfactual alternatives that did not occur. The completeness relation ensures that the ontic loop is always one of the N available eigenstates, and that the sum of probabilities |cₙ|² over all counterfactual and ontic loops equals unity.
