In semiclassical mechanics, a particle traces a closed orbit in phase space (x, p). The Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition requires that the enclosed area — the action integral ∮ p dx — equals an integer multiple of ħ. Each cell of area ħ represents one quantum state. Increase the quantum number n to see larger orbits enclosing more cells. The shape of the orbit depends on the potential: ellipses for the harmonic oscillator, rectangles for a particle in a box, and rounded pillows for a quartic potential.
