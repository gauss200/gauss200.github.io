In the coupled mode picture of quantum mechanics, the forward-time wavefunction ψ and reverse-time conjugate ψ* propagate in opposite directions along the z-axis (representing time). The coupling κ transfers amplitude between them. Measurements at the two ends act as boundary conditions: the initial measurement reflects ψ* into ψ, and the final measurement reflects ψ into ψ*. A physical solution requires round-trip self-consistency — the amplitude must return to itself after a complete loop through both boundary conditions. This is the retrocausal consistency condition: the future measurement constrains the past state, not through spacelike signaling, but through the requirement that forward and reverse propagation form a closed self-consistent loop.
