Two spin-½ particles created in a singlet state diverge along spacelike-separated worldlines. A spin-up measurement on particle A along axis θ instantaneously constrains the statistics of particle B. The conditional probability P(↑) = sin²((φ − θ)/2) shows perfect anti-correlation when φ = θ and no correlation when φ ⊥ θ — the signature of quantum entanglement that violates Bell's inequality.
