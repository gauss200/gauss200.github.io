The divergence theorem and Stokes' theorem both arise from the same principle: summing a derivative over a volume reduces to an integral over the boundary because interior contributions cancel between adjacent cells. Left: normal (divergence) arrows cancel, leaving boundary flux. Right: tangential (curl) arrows cancel, leaving boundary circulation.
