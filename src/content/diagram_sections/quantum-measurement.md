In the coupled mode theory of measurement, a system waveguide carrying |ψ⟩ is coupled to an initially empty measurement waveguide through an interaction operator A. As the modes co-propagate through the interaction region, amplitude builds up in the measurement channel. The coupling strength κ and interaction length L determine whether this is a weak measurement (minimal back-action) or a strong projective measurement (significant state transfer).
