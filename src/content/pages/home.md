## About

Warren Bruce Jackson received his PhD in Physics from UC Berkeley (1981), where he co-developed photothermal deflection spectroscopy (PDS) — a technique now widely used for characterizing sub-bandgap absorption in semiconductors.

Over a 40+ year career at Xerox PARC and HP Labs, his research has spanned the fundamental physics of hydrogenated amorphous silicon, metastable defect formation (Staebler-Wronski effect), hydrogen diffusion, thin-film transistors, flat-panel displays, photovoltaics, and machine learning.

He was elected a Fellow of the American Physical Society in 1995 for "pioneering research in the fundamental properties of amorphous semiconductors."
